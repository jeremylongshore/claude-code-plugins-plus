## The Problem
Anyone can use free plugins, but paying sponsors need recognition for their investment and custom development partnerships.

## Key Differentiators to Emphasize

### For Paying Sponsors:
1. **Investment Language**
   - "invests in" vs "uses"
   - "sponsors" vs "adopts"
   - "commissions" vs "downloads"

2. **Exclusivity Indicators**
   - "Custom-built for [Company]"
   - "Proprietary workflows"
   - "Tailored to enterprise needs"

3. **Partnership Status**
   - "Development partner since [date]"
   - "GitHub Sponsor at Enterprise tier"
   - "Strategic technology partner"

4. **Impact Statements**
   - "Funding community development"
   - "Enabling open-source sustainability"
   - "Driving innovation roadmap"

### What They Get (Explicit Benefits):
- Custom plugin development
- Priority feature requests
- Private/proprietary plugins
- Direct developer access
- Influence on roadmap
- Brand visibility as partner

### What They Enable (Community Impact):
- Sustainable open-source development
- Free plugins for everyone
- Continuous improvements
- Documentation and tutorials
- Community support
